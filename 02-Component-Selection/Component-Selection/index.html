    Humidity / Temperature Sensors

    Image Solution (with price) Pros Cons Link to Product
    SHT31-DIS-F2.5KS — $5.39/unit - 2.15–5.5 V voltage range (perfect)
    - Measures both humidity and temperature
    - In stock and good delivery time
    - High accuracy / good stability    		 - More expensive
    - Needy with maintenance    		 Link to product
    BMP384 — $3.35/unit - Low power consumption
    - Compact package    		 - Primarily designed for drones
    - Some features not a primary focus for the product in development    		 Link to product
    MPR121QR2 — $0.59/unit - Multiple channels (supports up to 12 electrodes)
    - Sleep/standby modes    		 - Comes in bulk orders only
    - Limited availability    		 Link to product

    Choice: Option 1 — SHT31-DIS-F2.5KS
    Rationale: This sensor best matches the project requirements. Despite its higher cost, it offers high accuracy, excellent stability, and dual humidity/temperature readings that provide valuable user data.

    Voltage Regulator

    Image Solution (with price) Pros Cons Link to Product
    TPS63030DSKR — $2.14/unit - Recommended for 2–3 AA battery-powered designs
    - Well-documented buck-boost solution    		 - More layout parts than some alternatives
    - Layout could become bulky    		 Link to product
    ISL9120IRTAZ-T7A — $2.44/unit - Very compact
    - Fewer layout parts needed    		 - Smaller size makes soldering more difficult Link to product
    RT5707 / RT5707A — $1.15/unit - Efficient buck converter
    - Low quiescent current (preserves battery life in sleep)    		 - No buck-boost (fails if VIN < VOUT)
    - Voltage range limited vs others    		 Link to product

    Choice: Option 1 — TPS63030DSKR
    Rationale: Well-suited for a three-AA-battery design. It meets the system’s power requirements and offers strong efficiency at a reasonable price, despite requiring a slightly larger layout.

    OLED Screen

    Image Solution (with price) Pros Cons Link to Product
    DIY0141 0.96 Inch OLED Display Blue/Yellow — $9.99 (2 units) - I²C connectivity (compatible with PIC)
    - Fastest delivery (2 days)
    - Good deal (comes with 2 units)
    - 3.3–5 V Voltage
    - 2 colors    		 - Slightly bulky compared to other OLEDs Link to product
    U602602 — $6.99/unit - I²C connectivity (compatible with PIC)
    - 3.3–5 V Voltage    		 - Longer delivery time
    - Only one unit per order
    - Most expensive per unit    		 Link to product
    YELUFT B0FKYVZ9W8 — $15.99 (5 OLEDs) / $8.99 (2 OLEDs) - I²C connectivity (compatible with PIC)
    - 2nd fastest delivery (5 days)
    - Best deal per unit
    - 3.3–5 V Voltage    		 - Slightly longer delivery
    - Only one color    		 Link to product

    Choice: Option 3 — YELUFT B0FKYVZ9W8
    Rationale: Provides the best value per unit and meets all compatibility needs. Its compact shape saves space, making it the most practical option for this design.

    MOSFET

    Image Solution (with price) Pros Cons Link to Product
    NTR4101PT1G P-CH 20 V 1.8 A SOT23-3 — $0.36/unit - Good balance between specs
    - Easy to solder    		 - Slightly wider than other MOSFETs Link to product
    BSS84 P-CH 50 V 130 mA SOT23-3 — $0.0798/unit - Cheapest option
    - Can handle up to 50 V (overkill but functional)    		 - Overspecified for the intended application Link to product
    DMP3068L-7 P-CH 30 V 3.3 A SOT23 — $0.36/unit - High current switch (~700 mW, overkill) - Bulkier compared to other MOSFETs Link to product

    Choice: Option 1 — NTR4101PT1G P-CH 20 V 1.8 A SOT23-3
    Rationale: Offers the most balanced performance without excessive current or voltage capacity. Ideal for controlling the OLED’s power consumption efficiently.
